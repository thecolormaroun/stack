# Dieter Rams: 10 Principles of Good Design

## 1. Good design is innovative
The possibilities for innovation are not exhausted. Technology always offers new opportunities for innovative design.

**In UI:** Don't just copy patterns. Consider if there's a better way.

## 2. Good design makes a product useful
A product is bought to be used. It has to satisfy certain criteria, not only functional, but also psychological and aesthetic.

**In UI:** Features exist to solve problems. Cut what doesn't serve the user.

## 3. Good design is aesthetic
The aesthetic quality of a product is integral to its usefulness because products we use every day affect our wellbeing.

**In UI:** Beauty matters. Users prefer tools that feel good.

## 4. Good design makes a product understandable
It clarifies the product's structure. Better still, it can make the product talk. At best, it is self-explanatory.

**In UI:** If you need a manual, the design failed. Affordances should be obvious.

## 5. Good design is unobtrusive
Products fulfilling a purpose are like tools. They are neither decorative objects nor works of art. Their design should be both neutral and restrained.

**In UI:** The interface should disappear. Content and tasks are primary.

## 6. Good design is honest
It does not make a product more innovative, powerful, or valuable than it really is. It does not attempt to manipulate the consumer with promises that cannot be kept.

**In UI:** Don't oversell. Don't use dark patterns. Be clear about limitations.

## 7. Good design is long-lasting
It avoids being fashionable and therefore never appears antiquated. Unlike fashionable design, it lasts many years.

**In UI:** Avoid trends that will date. Classic patterns endure.

## 8. Good design is thorough down to the last detail
Nothing must be arbitrary or left to chance. Care and accuracy in the design process show respect towards the user.

**In UI:** Edge cases matter. Loading states matter. Error states matter.

## 9. Good design is environmentally friendly
Design makes an important contribution to the preservation of the environment. It conserves resources and minimizes physical and visual pollution throughout the lifecycle.

**In UI:** Performance is environmental. Less JS, faster loads, lower energy.

## 10. Good design is as little design as possible
Less, but better. Back to purity, back to simplicity.

**In UI:** Remove until it breaks. Every element must earn its place.

---

## Application to Code Review

| Principle | Question to Ask |
|-----------|----------------|
| Innovative | Is there a better pattern we're not using? |
| Useful | Does every feature solve a real problem? |
| Aesthetic | Does this feel good to use? |
| Understandable | Can a new user figure this out? |
| Unobtrusive | Does the UI get out of the way? |
| Honest | Are we being truthful about capabilities? |
| Long-lasting | Will this age well? |
| Thorough | Are edge cases handled? |
| Environmentally friendly | Is this performant? |
| As little as possible | What can we remove? |
